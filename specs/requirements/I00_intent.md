One team, One agenda, One priority 

1. Specs, BOM & Engineering Definition Focus on how early and stable technical definitions enable reliable execution:

Clarity on BOM maturity, spec freeze points, and engineering handovers
Alignment on what must be defined early vs. what can evolve without risk
Reinforcing discipline on change management and technical ownership
2. Material Management & Sourcing Focus on securing supply and anticipating constraints:

Identification of critical components with lead times >8 weeks
Improving forecast visibility to suppliers (short‑ and mid‑term horizon)
Strengthening supplier commitment through better anticipation and governance
3. Manufacturing & Execution on the Floor Focus on turning plans into on‑time delivery:

Daily management and visual control of missing parts and execution risks
Earlier detection of deviations (from engineering release to assembly & testing)
Stronger link between technical definition, material availability, and shop-floor reality
Sharing and replicating proven practices already working in some sites
Across all sessions, teams addressed root causes, not symptoms: governance gaps, unclear ownership, lack of anticipation, and execution discipline. The exchanges were direct, sometimes challenging, but always constructive, with a clear intent to converge toward shared priorities.

The outcome is not a slide deck, but:

A short list of prioritized actions
Clear owners across EP&I and Supply Chain
A shared understanding of what must change now vs. what will be built over time
